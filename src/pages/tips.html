Habit Tips

10 science-backed strategies from coaches and psychologists to build lasting positive habits.

01
🌅

Start in the Morning

Morning routines set your day's rhythm. Research shows habits formed in the AM stick 50 days faster. Try: water, meditation, or making your bed.

→ Add one morning habit
02
🔮

Visualize Future You

Psychologists confirm: imagining your future self strengthens willpower today. Ask: "Will Future Me thank me for this choice?"

→ Picture your goal daily
03
🪶

Take Tiny Steps

Stanford's BJ Fogg: big results come from tiny, regular actions. One push-up daily beats 50 once. Your brain will crave more.

→ Shrink your habit to 2 minutes
04
🎯

Focus on Identity

James Clear: "You don't rise to your goals, you fall to your systems." Think "I am a runner" not "I want to run 5K".

→ Choose identity-based action
05
⏳

Give Yourself Time

Studies show habit formation takes 18-254 days (avg: 66). Your timeline is unique. Focus on joy in the process, not stressful deadlines.

→ Celebrate showing up
06
🛋️

Make It Convenient

Remove friction: place water on your desk, prep workout clothes the night before. Psychologist Wendy Wood slept in running gear to make jogging effortless.

→ Eliminate one obstacle
07
📝

Remember Your Why

Therapist Amy Morin: stress triggers old habits. Keep a visible list of your top 10 reasons for this habit. Review it when motivation dips.

→ Write your "Why" list
08
🔗

Anchor to a Trigger

Link new habits to existing routines: "After I brush my teeth, I will meditate". Triggers automate behavior without willpower.

→ Pick your trigger today
09
🤝

Get Support

Your environment shapes habits. Join a "success team" like coach Barbara Sher suggests. Share progress, celebrate wins together.

→ Invite a habit buddy
10
🎁

Reward Yourself

Stephen Covey: rewards reinforce new behavior. After completing your habit, pause and acknowledge: "I did it!" Your brain loves positive feedback.

→ Plan a small celebration
